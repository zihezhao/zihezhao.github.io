RayVio announced today that it has closed a $9 million dollar

Series B round of funding.

Feburuary 18, 2015

RayVio announced today that it has closed a $9 million dollar Series B round of funding, supported by DCM Ventures, Applied Materials Ventures and additional private investors. The funding will enable RayVio to support its accelerated go-to-market strategy and global offering of UV LED solutions for the global water and air disinfection, medical treatment, and UV curing markets.

 

“We are excited about our series B round which includes DCM and Applied Ventures and our private investors,” said Dr. Robert Walker, CEO of RayVio. “The market and our customers have shown tremendous interest in our UV LED solutions. As a result of our industry leading performance and execution, we are currently oversubscribed, with the potential for RayVio to receive additional funding in future rounds.”

 

RayVio’s UV LED solutions provide the UV industry with up to a 10x increase in performance over existing UV LED solutions today, enabling RayVio’s customers to provide best-in-class UV solutions. RayVio’s UV LED performance will enable the mass deployment of UV LED solutions to consumers in a host of applications ranging from solar powered UV water disinfection water pitchers for clean water stressed developing economies around the world, to hand-held UV disinfection modules capable of disinfecting any surface from touchscreen devices, to counter tops, to doorknobs, to airplane tray tables.

 

“We are witnessing an explosion of new opportunities, in a variety of markets in the UV LED industry, “ said Dr. Andreas Kolch, Managing Director and Partner Hytecon GmbH. “With RayVio’s UV LED performance realized, the UV LED industry will be able to deploy a wide offering of world class UV solutions that would otherwise not be available for years to come.”

 

Until now, using LEDs for UV applications has not been possible due to low performance, high cost and compromised reliability. RayVio’s novel, patent protected materials and technology, originally developed at Boston University by co-founders Dr. Yitao Liao and Dr. Theodore Moustakas, unleash Haitz’s Law in UV LEDs, the same Haitz’s Law that made the mass deployment of cost effective visual LEDs possible in the display and lighting markets.

 

“We’re excited to see the customer feedback from industry leaders in a variety of billion dollar markets” said Pete Moran of DCM Ventures. “RayVio’s customers and partners are developing industry best, high-performance, cost competitive solutions that will be commercially available later this year.”

 

RayVio continues to gain global recognition for their performance achievements across a variety of UV markets. RayVio, it’s partners and customers, will release a series of product announcements later this year.
