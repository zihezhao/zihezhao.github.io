Cannabis & Cancer

The cannabinoids found in cannabis act through the body’s natural endocannabinoid system to cure and/or treat cancer in 4 key ways:

 

• Prevents cancer cells from reproducing, which is fundamentally the biggest issue with cancer cells. (Antiproliferative Effects of Cannabis).

• Prevents the formation of new blood vessels needed by tumors to grow; tumors can no longer be supported. (Antiangiogenic Effects of Cannabis).

• Prevents cancer from spreading to other organs, acting as a biological shield.

• (Antimetastatic Effects of Cannabis).

• Seeks out and induces cancerous cells to commit aptosis: cancer cells die, healthy cells live. In fact, the healthy cells are specifically protected by cannabinoids like THC and Cancerous cells continue to produce CB2 receptors, which act as a target for the cannabinoids found in cannabis.

• (Apoptotic Effects of Cannabis).

Major Cancer Studies

• A ﻿Harvard study released on April 17, 2007 shows that the active ingredient in marijuana, THC, cuts tumor growth in common lung cancer in half and significantly reduces the ability of the cancer to spread. Link to article.

 

• A pair of scientists at California Pacific Medical Center in San Francisco have found after over 20 years of research that a compound derived from cannabis could stop metastasis in many kinds of aggressive cancer, potentially altering the fatality of the disease forever: ﻿"We started by researching breast cancer," said Pierre Desprez, one of the scientists behind the discovery. "But now we've found that Cannabidiol works with many kinds of aggressive cancers--brain, prostate. The pair combined CBD and cells containing high levels of ID-1, the gene that causes cancer to spread, in a petri dish. "What we found was that his Cannabidiol could essentially 'turn off' the ID-1," Desprez told HuffPost. The cells stopped spreading and returned to normal.

 

• Cannabis has proven extremely useful for mitigating some of the negative side effects of radiation and chemotherapy. Delta-9 THC is legally prescribed for counteracting the severe side effects of these cancer therapies, and is prescribed by doctors under its trade name, Marinol, which is taken orally via a pill. The paradox of swallowing a pill to eliminate vomiting has been noted by many physicians and patients, as well as the American Medical Association. Smoking cannabis has proven a quicker and more effective option than oral pills. Link to National Cancer Institute Fact Sheet.

 

• In 2008 the Leukimia and Lymphoma Society released a formal statement calling for the end of civil and criminal sanctions for seriously ill patients advised to use medical cannabis by licensed doctors. The Leukemia and Lymphoma Society is the second largest cancer charity in the U.S., and the world's largest voluntary health organization dedicated to funding blood cancer research, education and patient services. Cannabis & Cancer Patents

A US patent published on March 7, 2013 filed by GW Pharma shows the use of phytocannabinoids in the treatment of various forms of cancer. Typically the cancer to be treated is a cancer of the: prostate, breast, skin, glioma, colon, lung or a bone or lymph metastasis. The patent indicates that researchers have discovered that some cannabinoids, including THC and CBD are able to promote the re-emergence of apoptosis (the signal for a cell to die) so that some tumors will heed the signals, stop dividing, and die. Link to Patent. 

 

Another US patent published on May 11, 2011 filed by GW Pharma shows the use of a combination of cannabinoids, particularily THC and CBD, in the treatment of cancer. The patent states that it is the different permutations and combinations of the cannabinoids that are important in the effectiveness of the treatment, specifically the patent shows the treatment of brain tumors. Link to Patent. 

Cannabis & Lung Cancer

Smoking cannabis does not in fact lead to an increased risk in lung cancer. The largest study of its kind has unexpectedly concluded that smoking marijuana, even regularly and heavily, does not lead to lung cancer. The new findings "were against our expectations," said Donald Tashkin of the University of California at Los Angeles, a pulmonologist who has studied marijuana for 30 years. "We hypothesized that there would be a positive association between marijuana use and lung cancer, and that the association would be more positive with heavier use," he said. "What we found instead was no association at all, and even a suggestion of some protective effect." Link to article.
