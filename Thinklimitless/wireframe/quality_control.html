Quality Control

Standardization or quality control is essential to the long-term success of cannabis as a medical treatment. The availability of cannabis testing allows for better access to medically important cannabis options such as CBD-rich cannabis. Laboratory testing also helps promote the overall legitimization of cannabis for therapeutic use. Right now the cannabis testing industry is unregulated.

Access to Testing

 

Central testing labs play a key role in testing today.  We hope that in the coming years, the following practical challenges can be overcome in order to allow these labs to test all cannabis that is available for sale for consumers. 

 

• A chain of custody is required and samples must be picked up or dropped off in person at a central testing facility with complex paperwork.

• Requires a labor intensive technical lab workup to extract chemicals prior to analysis by capital intensive large capital equipment (HPLC/GC).

• Expensive for smaller batches: $60-$300/sample (depending on level of testing) + 1-4g sample.

• Lack of easy access to central labs and higher costs result in some dispensaries reusing labels to save costs (not delivering accurate analysis data to customers), making matters worse for all stakeholders, mainly patients.​​
