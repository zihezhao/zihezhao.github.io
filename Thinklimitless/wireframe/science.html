Science

There are over 100 chemicals in Cannabis. It's not just about THC.  Most of them fall into three categories: cannabinoids, terpenoids, and flavanoids.

 

Each of these compounds can significantly modify the therapeutic benefits of the plant. There are hundreds of different chemical combinations each producing a different effect. Some of these effects and interactions have yet to be discovered!

Meet the Cannabinoids

 

CBD-Cannabidiol

CBD has been found to give the most medical benefits of all the different chemicals found in cannabis. CBD can also decrease the social isolation characteristics introduced by THC(Tetrahydrocannabinol). CBD also has low psychoactive properties making it ideal for medical use.

 

Studies have shown CBD:

• Helps control certain cancers

• Helps with controlling pain

• Stimulates bone growth

• Suppresses muscle spasms and convulsions

• Slows inflammation

• Helps with nausea

• Reduces the risk of artery obstructions

• Decrases pressure in the blood vessel walls

• Reduces blood sugar levels

• Assists in controlling epileptic seizures

• Helps reduce risk of nerve damage

• Decreases the social isolation caused by THC

THC-Tetrahydrocannabinol

 

THC has been shown to have a wide range of medical benefits. THC is most associated with the high and euphoria feeling when using cannabis. THC has very high psychoactive characteristics associated with it. Over medicating with THC can cause adverse side effects.

 

Studies have shown THC:

• Helps with controlling pain

• Helps with relaxation

• Suppresses pain from nerve damage

• Helps reduce risk of nerve damage

• Helps control anxiety

• Suppresses muscle spasms and convulsions

• Helps control certain cancers

• Helps with nausea

• Slows inflamation

• Helps fight free radicals in the blood stream

• Encourages eating and appetite stimulation

• Stimulates new growth in nerve tissue

• Relieves chronic eye pressure and pain caused from glaucoma and other eye disorders

CBN-Cannabinol

 

There is very little CBN present in fresh cannabis plants. The more CBN present means the less THC. Cannabis containing higher levels of CBN indicate older age and improper handling techniques. CBN has mildly psychoactive characteristics associated to it. High CBN levels have aslos shown undesirable symptoms like confusion, lightheadedness, and acts as a weak agonist of the cannabinoid receptors.

 

Studies have shown CBN:

• Acts as a sleep aid

• Slows inflammation

• Helps with controlling pain

• Suppresses muscle spasms and convulsions

• Helps fight free radicals in the blood stream

Terpenes/Terpinoids

 

Terpinoids are the main component of any plant resin or essential oils and play many important roles in plants- from deterring insect predation, protection from environmental stresses and importantly, as chemical building blocks for more complex molecules, like cannabinoids. Terpinoids are responsible for the aromatic qualities of plants. There are over 120 kinds of terpenes in cannabis, some only in trace amounts with others in double-digit percentage. 

 

The main terpinoids in cannabis:

 

• Beta-caryophyllene (BCP). According to Berkley Patients Care, BCP is the terpene responsible for activating the CB2 receptor and acts as a non-psychoactive anti-inflammatory.

• Limonene. Its' potent anti-carcinogenic and anti-fungal properties, are speculated to be the component protecting marijuana smokers from carcinogens and fungi found in cannabis smoke.

• ﻿Myrcene. Has an effect on the permeability of cell membranes, which allows for the absorption of more cannabinoids by brain cells.
