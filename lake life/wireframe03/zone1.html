BEYOND THE ZONE

The Norris Highlands are rich in the traditions of Appalachian culture. An emerald lake, wide open spaces, a rich heritage of culture, music and history await you in this East Tennessee vacation playground. Norris Lake is perfect for boating, cliff diving, scuba diving, camping and fishing. There are 20+ full service marinas offering rentals and 60+ boat ramps for those who want to bring their own boats. Relax in the peace and quiet of the area parks, wildlife, museums, festivals, crafts and music. Kick up your feet, relax in a resort, dine on delicious regional food and experience a sunset framed by the mountains.

Find your Zone On and Beyond | Norris Lake Tennessee

You’ll find the perfect combination of mountain and lake scenery, water sports recreation historic tradition, all served up by friendly, hard-working people with a keen sense of volunteerism and business enterprise. Framed by the Cumberland Mountains, Norris Lake and four prestine state parks.

Caryville, Jacksboro, Jellico & LaFollette | Campbell County

A great place to call home and enjoy a quiet, relaxing lifestyle. Anderson County delivers what many areas only promise: small town life amid forested hills, with large, sparkling lakes and clean rivers. The municipalities of Clinton, Lake City, Norris, Oak Ridge, and Oliver Springs, together with rural areas, reflect a diverse county, strongly influenced by its heritage and history, but deeply committed to the bright future that lies ahead.dio.

Andersonville, Clinton, Lake City & Norris | Anderson & Union County

Union County, home to legendary and popular musicians; home to families with deep roots and an appreciation of the rich cultural history of the area; home to retirees from many areas of the country who have discovered the unique beauty and recreational opportunities that are in abundance; home to tourists that annually visit our parks and pristine waterways and shorelines. Union County has something for everyone with a deep emerald lake nestled in the shadows of the Appalachian Mountains, museums, historic landmarks, quaint shops, and wide open spaces perfect for viewing wildlife or enjoying an outdoor adventure. Combined with a mild climate and the perfect location, come see why so many people choose to call Union County home!

Maynardville & Sharps Chapel, Big Ridge State Park & Chuck Swan Wildlife Area | Campbell & Union County

Come and visit us and explore the area! Claiborne County is a unique blend of tourism, history, natural resources and a thriving business community. Water lovers will delight in Claiborne County's boating, swimming, camping, hiking and canoeing on and around Norris Lake. Along the 800+ miles of shoreline there are two state wildlife management areas. Claiborne County is also proud of the excellent higher educational opportunities within the county. Walters State Community College and Lincoln Memorial University, nestled within the pristine Appalachian Mountain range, founded more than 100 years ago, provides the opportunity to pursue and fulfill a college degree. LMU campus is the home of the Abraham Lincoln Library and Museum houses one of the most diverse Abraham Lincoln and Civil War collections in the country.

Tazewell & New Tazewell | Claiborne County

Claiborne County, Tennessee, home to the historic Cumberland Gap passage, and the easily manageable trail to "tri-state point" where Kentucky, Tennessee and Virgina converge. Much of the county's early history surrounds the Cumberland Gap, founded in 1801, which was the important Indian trail long before the area was settled. Once called "The Gateway to the West" the mountain pass has been an important trade route for over 200 years. Cumberland Gap National Park offers a wide variety of activities that compliments the area for all nature and history enthusiasts. Appreciate the historical significance the Gap and imagine the area as it was explored in 1750 by Dr. Thomas Walker and Daniel Boone in 1769. Hiking the almost 70 miles of trails, visiting the Pinnacle Overlook (altitude of 2,400 ft.), exploring Gap Cave or the ever popular Hensley Settlement is always a rewarding experience. On and Beyond the Lake, Claiborne and Union County are a year-round diverse destination.

Cumberland Gap, Harrogate & Speedwell | Claiborne & Union County

The East Tennessee Region is know for its pristine lakes and mountains, but each corner has claim to fame in its own right. Like pieces of a puzzle, each community and county add to the overall image of a population demonstrating the American pioneer spirit on a daily basis. Discover East Tennessee in a whole new way. Explore the back roads, visiting local gems such as wineries, historic main streets, quaint restaurants, scenic spots for outdoor adventure and so much more. Embrace the history that makes this area famous: Civil War, Moonshine & Nascar, Music, Native American Heritage and of course the Appalachia Mountains and Valleys, filled with good old Southern hospitality. You just can't go wrong, y'all!
